## 2. About the Company: Garena

Garena is **a game publisher, not a game developer**. This distinction matters enormously for how the role works:

- Garena **licenses games** from external developers (e.g., FC Online is licensed from Nexon Korea) and publishes them in Southeast Asia and Taiwan
- Garena's job is to **operate the game in-market**: run it, grow it, monetize it, and serve the player community
- Garena does **not build the core game engine or features** — that's the developer's job
- What Garena *does* build and own: the local player experience, events, pricing, community, and go-to-market strategy

**Implication for Coby's role**: The work is fundamentally about **business operations and player engagement**, not software development. Decisions are about how to run the game, not how to build it.

Garena operates in Vietnam as part of a broader regional structure. Country-level decisions are made by the **Country Head**, with **Game Leaders (Product Management Leaders)** owning individual game titles.

---

## 3. About the Game: FC Online

**FC Online** (formerly FIFA Online) is a **PC-based free-to-play football simulation game**, published by Garena in Vietnam. Key characteristics:

- One of Vietnam's most played PC games with a large, loyal player base
- Monetization model: **free to play, pay for upgrades** — players spend money on player cards (called "packs"), boosts, cosmetics, etc.
- Heavily tied to the **real football calendar**: major events often align with Champions League, World Cup qualifiers, transfers, etc.
- Has a **competitive/esports dimension**: ranked play, in-game tournaments, and organized leagues
- Player community is **highly vocal and engaged** — forums, Facebook groups, Discord, YouTube content creators

**The game is a live service** — it never "launches and finishes." There are constant updates, events, balance patches, and seasonal cycles. This means the ops team is always running something.

## 4. The Role: Strategy & Operations (S&O)

### What this role is

S&O at Garena is a **rotational, high-ownership role** designed to develop well-rounded operators. It is not a specialized function — it sits **across functions**, taking on whatever the business needs most.

The role is directly shaped and guided by:
- **Country Head** (top of the Vietnam org)
- **Game Leaders / Product Management Leaders** (FC Online-specific leadership)

This means Coby will work on **real business problems with real stakes**, not internal projects or support tasks.

### Core Responsibilities

**1. Project Execution (Primary function)**
- Take a project from brief → plan → execution → result
- Manage timelines, coordinate stakeholders, remove blockers
- Own deliverables end-to-end with accountability for outcomes

**2. Cross-functional Coordination**
- Work across teams: game ops, product, community, business/monetization, and sometimes external partners
- Serve as the connective tissue between functions during a project

**3. Problem Solving**
- Diagnose business or operational problems (e.g., retention drop, underperforming event, player complaints)
- Develop solutions and execution plans, often with limited precedent

**4. Performance Tracking**
- Define success metrics per project
- Track, report, and interpret results for leadership
- Translate data into decisions

**5. Planning**
- Project planning: scope, milestones, dependencies, resource needs
- Strategic planning: contributing to game-level strategy and priorities (over time)

## 5. What "Projects" Actually Look Like

In the context of FC Online, the S&O role will touch these types of work:

### In-Game Events
The most frequent type of work. FC Online runs events constantly — seasonal (Tết, summer, back-to-school), football calendar-tied (World Cup, Champions League finals), and game-driven (new player card releases, anniversary events).

Each event involves:
- Defining the event mechanic (what players do, what they earn)
- Coordinating with the developer (Nexon) on feature implementation
- Planning the go-to-market (how to announce and promote — handled with marketing team)
- Operational execution (making sure the event runs correctly, monitoring in real-time)
- Post-event analysis (did it hit targets? what do we do next?)

### Tournaments & Esports
FC Online has a structured competitive scene. S&O may be involved in:
- Planning tournament formats and schedules
- Coordinating logistics (online brackets, offline event production)
- Liaising with community organizers and pro players
- Tracking participation and viewership metrics

### Offline/Community Events
Periodic larger activations — watch parties, fan events, meet-and-greets with pro players, etc. These involve external vendors, venue ops, and player community management.

### Business Problem Projects
These are less predictable but high-impact: if retention drops, if a competitor makes a move, if a monetization mechanic underperforms — leadership may task S&O to diagnose and propose solutions.
